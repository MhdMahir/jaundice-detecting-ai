About Jaundice Detection AI

Jaundice Detection AI is an intelligent healthcare support system designed to assist in the early detection of neonatal jaundice using image-based analysis. The system uses computer vision and machine learning to analyse visual cues from photos and provide fast, accessible screening support — even in resource-limited settings.

priority_high

Why Early Jaundice Detection Matters

Neonatal jaundice affects up to 60% of full-term newborns and 80% of preterm infants in the first week of life. While most cases are mild and resolve without treatment, high bilirubin levels left undetected can lead to serious neurological complications including kernicterus — a form of permanent brain damage. Early screening dramatically improves outcomes.

How the System Works

  • 01 A clear photo of the newborn (face or full body) is uploaded via any smartphone or browser.
  • 02 The image is sent to our backend, where a trained AI model processes it using computer vision.
  • 03 The model evaluates colour patterns in the skin and sclera (whites of the eyes) associated with bilirubin accumulation.
  • 04 A preliminary result (Positive / Negative) with a confidence score is returned in under 3 seconds.
psychology

Technology Behind the System

The project integrates a custom-trained convolutional neural network (CNN) built on a Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) architecture for robust feature extraction. The model was trained on a labelled dataset of neonatal images across diverse skin tones to maximise accuracy and fairness. The backend is built with Python (Flask), and the frontend communicates with the model via a REST API.

warning_amber

Important Disclaimer

This system is a decision-support tool only and does not replace professional medical diagnosis. Results should always be reviewed and confirmed by a qualified healthcare professional. In all cases of suspected jaundice, a formal serum bilirubin test is required.
